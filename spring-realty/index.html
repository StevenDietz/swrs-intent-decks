03 — Your questions, answered

The questions owners ask — answered

How it works, the rent-collection process, the paperwork we need, and what it costs.

Your questionSouthwest Recovery Services
1. How does it work?You place a past-due tenant account through our secure portal and outreach begins within 24–48 hours: professional, FDCPA-compliant demand across mail, phone, email and text; skip tracing when a tenant has moved on; payment plans when that’s what gets you paid; credit-bureau reporting; and pre-legal review for judgment-worthy balances. Pure contingency — $0 upfront, a pre-agreed percentage only of what we recover.
2. The rent-collection process, step by stepPlace the account with its file → we validate the balance against the lease and final ledger → locate and contact the former tenant → report the debt → negotiate payment in full or a structured plan → recovered funds are remitted with a full statement. You track every account in the portal, 24/7.
3. The paperwork we needLighter than most expect — per account: the signed lease, the final tenant ledger, the move-out statement or itemized damages, and the contact details from the rental application. The portal takes the files directly; no re-keying.
4. Credit reporting that tenants feelWe report to Equifax and Experian — including Experian RentBureau at no charge, the rental-specific bureau landlords and screening services pull. A reported balance follows a former tenant to their next rental application — the strongest lawful motivator to resolve it.
5. What it costsNothing upfront, ever. Property contingency is tiered by the age and size of the balance, which is why placing sooner is the single biggest recovery lever. We give an honest, age-based read on your accounts before anything is worked.

The cost of waiting

Why fresh tenant balances recover best

A move-out balance is most collectible in its first months, while contact data is warm and the tenant is applying for their next rental. Research from the Commercial Collection Agencies of America puts collectability at under 25% once a balance passes a year old. The highest-leverage change isn’t chasing harder — it’s placing sooner.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90 days from move-out) resolve fastest — the tenant is findable and motivated by their next application.

The standing rule

A fixed “place at 30–60 days after move-out” policy recovers materially more than case-by-case chasing — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.

Recovery figures vary by portfolio; SWRS gives an honest, age-based read on your accounts before anything is worked.
