03 — Your questions, answered

Your three questions — answered directly

Straight from your note, in order — plus the three things a 108-year firm will want to know next.

Your questionSouthwest Recovery Services
1. Do you handle individual / residential consumer accounts?Yes — consumer recovery is a core SWRS practice, not a sideline. Homeowner accounts are worked under the FDCPA and CFPB Regulation F in 47 states including New York, by collectors trained specifically for consumer contact. Our residential-trades book includes plumbing and home-services programs worked at hundreds-of-accounts scale.
2. What is your fee structure?Pure contingency — $0 upfront, no retainer, no monthly minimum. You pay a pre-agreed percentage only of what we actually recover; if we recover nothing, you owe nothing. The rate is quoted against a representative sample of your batch — account ages, balance sizes and documentation drive it — the same model you already know from your commercial agency, applied by a consumer specialist.
3. What does intake look like?Built for exactly your situation: a batch, ready to go. Export the accounts from your billing system (or send a spreadsheet) and upload through our secure client portal — we map your columns once, no re-keying. Outreach begins within 24–48 hours of a documented placement, and your team has live portal visibility from day one.
Why your files are strongMuch of your residential work originates in DEP enforcement — cease & desist orders, 3-day and 10-day notices — so the homeowner was required to order the repair, and the job is permitted and inspected. That paper trail neutralizes the most common consumer disputes and supports credit-bureau furnishing on qualifying accounts.

Will this reflect well on a 108-year name?It has to. Contact is professional, documented and fully compliant — no threats, no pressure tactics. Your debtors are one-time customers, but your name is on trucks all over four boroughs; we collect like we know it.

The age curve

Why completed-work balances recover best placed fresh

Look at the spread above: fresh, well-documented completed-work balances recover at 79–92 cents on the dollar; aged residential accounts placed late recover 20. The balance didn’t change — the trail did. Homeowners refinance, sell, and move; every month a batch sits, contact data decays and newer bills win.

The age curve

Balances placed within ~90–120 days of completion resolve at several times the rate of accounts left a year or more.

The standing rule

Place the existing batch now — it costs nothing on contingency — then set a standing rule: completed-work balances place at 60–90 days, every month, automatically.

05 — Built for contractor consumer receivables

We understand a completed-work ledger

A homeowner balance for finished trade work is a specific kind of consumer account. Our playbook is built for exactly that.

City-mandated completed work

DEP cease & desist, 3-day and 10-day notice jobs — the homeowner had to order the repair; the permit and inspection prove it was delivered. We work the file on that paper.

Sold-and-moved homeowners

Daily Experian-powered skip tracing locates former owners at their next address — the step that makes recovery possible at all.

Bureau-grade leverage

Qualifying accounts report to all three major credit bureaus — the consequence that brings a five-figure balance to the table before anyone mentions court.

Complement, not conflict

We take the consumer side; your commercial agency keeps the B2B side. Clean lanes, no overlap, and a 108-year brand protected by documented, compliant conduct.
