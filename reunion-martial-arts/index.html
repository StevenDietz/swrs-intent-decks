The cost of waiting

Why lapsed tuition recovers best while it’s fresh

A membership balance is most collectible while it’s fresh — the family’s contact and payment details are current, the enrollment is recent, and the relationship is intact. Every month it ages, people move, cards change, and the balance competes with newer bills. The highest-leverage move a school can make isn’t chasing harder — it’s handing it off sooner.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90–120 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates; balances left a year or more collect a fraction of that.

The standing rule

A simple “hand it off at 90 days” habit recovers more than letting balances drift toward write-off — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.
