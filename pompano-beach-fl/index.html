01 — Introduction

Turning uncollected EMS transport and non-tax revenue back into budget

Prepared for the City of Pompano Beach — Fire Rescue has run its own EMS transport 24/7 since 1975 across seven stations, and a coastal city treats and transports as many visitors as residents. Every transport is a bill, and the patient-responsibility balances left after insurance — especially for out-of-town patients — are among the hardest a city ever collects. Add delinquent utilities, code-enforcement fines, parking and permit fees, and the uncollected non-tax book adds up fast.

EMS transport is the marquee bleed. The city bills every ambulance transport through its patient portal, but a large share of patient responsibility after insurance goes unpaid — and visitors transported to the hospital leave with a balance and no local address. Those, plus delinquent utilities, fines and fees, accumulate quietly in an aging report and get written off.

Southwest Recovery Services recovers exactly this — patient EMS balances, delinquent utilities, code and permit fees — on pure contingency since 2004, worked the way a public-sector creditor should be represented: firm, professional, documented, and transparent enough to defend at a public meeting. EMS patient accounts run under HIPAA and the FDCPA. No recovery, no fee — no budget line to defend.

What we recoverUnpaid EMS / ambulance-transport balances (patient responsibility after insurance), delinquent water / sewer / solid-waste accounts, code-enforcement and administrative fines, parking citations, permit / inspection / business-license fees, false-alarm fees, returned checks and special assessments — any overdue balance owed to the city.
EMS transport & out-of-town patientsA beach city transports many non-residents; those patients leave with a balance and no local address, and the patient-responsibility portion after insurance often goes unworked. Public accountability & complianceWe work every account in firm, professional language consistent with how a public-sector creditor should be represented — no harassment, no threats, nothing that creates liability — documented thoroughly enough to defend at a public meeting or under an open-records request.
Which rules applyEMS patient balances are consumer debt handled under HIPAA and the FDCPA / Regulation F; resident utility, parking and court-cost balances under the FDCPA / Reg F; business and inter-governmental balances as commercial debt. We apply each framework to the right account and report under the FCRA.

Why place aged municipal debt

At zero cost to place, recovery is pure upside

Municipal non-tax debt is usually left to age because working it costs staff time the city doesn’t have — so it sits, then it’s written off. Contingency changes that calculus entirely.

No downside

On no-recovery-no-fee terms, placing an aged utility or EMS balance costs nothing; whatever comes back is net-new general-fund revenue.

Place early, recover more

Fresh balances recover far better than aged ones — a standing placement cadence on newly-delinquent accounts lifts the whole book over time.
