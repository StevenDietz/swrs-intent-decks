01 — Introduction

The recovery layer for what your billing process couldn’t close

Prepared for Metropolitan Houston Surgery Associates — for a surgical practice, net revenue leaks at the end of the revenue cycle: in the patient responsibility that surgery leaves behind, the self-pay and cash-procedure balances, and the denied, underpaid or prior-auth-stalled claims that survive billing and age past the point your office can work them.

This is the last stage of the revenue cycle, not the first. Coding, claim submission and adjudication are your billing team’s job; the balances that survive that process are where net revenue quietly leaks. A patient balance worked at 60 days resolves at a far higher rate than the same balance at 300.

Southwest Recovery Services is the recovery layer that works those balances — patient A/R, insurance follow-up and bad debt — HIPAA-compliant and on pure contingency since 2004. We resolve what billing couldn’t: no retainer, no setup fee, no recovery no fee. We complement your billing; we do not replace it.

The early-out advantage

Why early placement recovers more

For patient AR, the age of the balance is the strongest predictor of recovery — contact data goes cold and patients disengage as accounts age.

The age curve

Fresh balances recover a large multiple of aged ones; the decline is steep and does not reverse.

Early-out + bad debt

An early-out program on fresh balances plus prompt bad-debt placement, worked in parallel, recovers materially more than a single late hand-off.
