01 — Introduction

A recovery partner for the resident balances that age out

To Stephanie Heitman and the Seven Acres business office — for more than 80 years Seven Acres has cared for Houston’s seniors as a mission-driven, not-for-profit community. The hardest dollars a community like yours collects are the resident balances that linger after the care is delivered: private-pay balances, the Medicaid-pending gap, Medicare co-insurance, and responsible-party obligations that go quiet once a resident is discharged.

Aged resident A/R isn’t a business-office problem — it’s a mission problem. For a not-for-profit senior-care community, every dollar recovered from a written-off resident balance is a dollar back toward care — and on a Medicare / Medicaid / private-pay book, those balances add up quickly across a 100+-bed census.

Southwest Recovery Services works patient and resident receivables on pure contingency since 2004 — licensed and bonded where required, including Texas, and HIPAA-aware in how we handle resident information. Recovery is dignity-first: firm, professional, and respectful of the resident, the family, and the Seven Acres name. You place accounts through a secure portal that takes a resident-A/R aging export, and we work them. No recovery, no fee.

The cost of waiting

Why resident balances recover best while they’re fresh

Results vary widely by how aged the file is when it’s placed. The strongest senior-care recoveries above came from balances placed while they were still fresh — the resident in-house or recently discharged, contact and responsible-party details current. Every month a balance ages, families scatter and leverage erodes. The highest-leverage change a business office can make isn’t chasing harder — it’s placing sooner.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90–120 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates — the 40–100% recoveries above — while balances left a year or more, especially after discharge, collect a fraction of that.

The standing rule

A simple “place at 90 days” policy recovers materially more than letting resident balances drift toward write-off — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.

05 — Built for senior-care & skilled-nursing A/R

We understand a senior-care ledger

A Medicare / Medicaid / private-pay resident book creates a specific receivables profile. Our playbook is built for exactly that.

Private-pay resident balances

Monthly room-and-board and ancillary balances that fall to the resident or family — pursued professionally while they are still collectible.

The Medicaid-pending gap

Care delivered before eligibility is approved — and applications that lapse or deny — leave stranded balances. We recover the portion that becomes resident or responsible-party debt.

Co-insurance, non-covered & discharged

Medicare Part A co-insurance (days 21–100), non-covered days, and balances that go quiet once a resident is discharged — the hardest to collect in-house, and exactly what we work.

Dignity-first & HIPAA-aware

Empathetic, compliant contact with residents and responsible parties that protects a mission-driven, community-rooted nonprofit’s name.
