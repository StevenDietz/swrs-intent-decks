The cost of waiting

Why patient balances recover best while they’re fresh

A patient balance is most collectible while it’s fresh — the work or sale is recent, contact details are current, and the customer remembers what they owe. Every month it ages, people move, numbers change, and the balance competes with newer bills. The highest-leverage move isn’t chasing harder — it’s handing it off sooner.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90–120 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates; balances left a year or more collect a fraction of that.

The standing rule

A simple “place at 90 days” habit recovers more than letting balances drift toward write-off — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.
