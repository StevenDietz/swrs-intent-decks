The cost of waiting

Why unpaid cleans recover best while they’re fresh

A balance is most collectible while the job is fresh — the clean was recent, the customer’s contact details are current, and they remember the work you did. Every month it ages, people move, numbers change, and the balance competes with newer bills. The highest-leverage move a busy owner can make isn’t chasing harder — it’s handing it off sooner.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90–120 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates; balances left a year or more collect a fraction of that.

The standing rule

A simple “hand it off at 90 days” habit recovers more than letting balances drift toward write-off — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.
