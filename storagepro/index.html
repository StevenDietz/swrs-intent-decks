The cost of waiting

Why storage balances recover best while they’re fresh

A storage balance is most collectible while it’s fresh — the tenant’s contact and employment details are current, the lien file is complete, and the account is recent. Every month it ages, people relocate, numbers change, and the trail goes cold. The highest-leverage move a portfolio operator can make isn’t chasing harder — it’s placing sooner, consistently, across every site.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90–120 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates; deficiency balances left a year or more after auction collect a fraction of that.

The standing rule

A standardized “place at 90 days” policy across the portfolio recovers more than letting each site’s balances drift toward write-off — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.
