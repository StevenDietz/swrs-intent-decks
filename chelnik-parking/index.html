The cost of waiting

Why parking balances recover best while they’re fresh

A monthly-account balance is most collectible while it’s fresh — the parker’s contact and payment details are current, the account is recent, and the relationship is intact. Every month it ages, people move, cards change, and the balance competes with newer bills. The highest-leverage move an operator can make isn’t chasing harder — it’s handing it off sooner.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90–120 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates; balances left a year or more collect a fraction of that once the parker is gone.

The standing rule

A simple “hand it off at 90 days” habit recovers more than letting balances drift toward write-off — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.
