The cost of waiting

Why lease arrears recover best while they’re fresh

A commercial balance is most collectible while it’s fresh — the tenant is still operating, the guaranty is enforceable, and the lease and ledger are at hand. Every month it ages, businesses close, principals scatter, and leverage erodes. The highest-leverage change a landlord can make isn’t chasing harder — it’s placing sooner.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90–120 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates; balances left a year or more — especially after a tenant closes — collect a fraction of that.

The standing rule

A simple “place at 90 days” policy on delinquent and former-tenant balances recovers more than letting them drift toward write-off — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.
