The cost of waiting

Why legal-fee balances recover best while they’re fresh

A fee balance is most collectible while the matter is fresh — the representation is recent, contact details are current, and the client still associates the balance with the work you did. Every month it ages, people move, memories fade, and the balance competes with newer bills. The highest-leverage change a firm can make isn’t chasing harder — it’s placing sooner.

The age curve

Fresh balances (under ~90–120 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates; balances left a year or more collect a fraction of that.

The standing rule

A simple “place at 90 days” policy recovers more than letting balances drift toward write-off — and on contingency there’s no cost to placing.
