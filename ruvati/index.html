The cost of waiting

Why commercial balances recover best while they’re fresh

A trade balance is most collectible while it’s fresh — the relationship is recent, the paper is current, and the debtor entity still exists where you last saw it. Every month it ages, dealers close, principals move, and the balance competes with every other creditor. The highest-leverage move isn’t chasing harder — it’s placing sooner.

The age curve

Fresh accounts (under ~90 days past due) resolve at materially higher rates; balances left a year or more collect a fraction of that.

The standing rule

A fixed “place at 60–90 days” policy on trade accounts recovers materially more than an annual cleanup — and on contingency there is no cost to placing.
