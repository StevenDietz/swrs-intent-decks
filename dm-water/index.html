01 — Introduction

A recovery partner for the final bills that leave with your members

To Ms. Mobley and the D & M board — thank you for your detailed inquiry. A member-owned water supply corporation carries a receivable most businesses never see: the unpaid final bill of a member who moved on — small balances, often with no forwarding address, multiplying quietly across roughly 2,300 meters and 140 square miles of service area.

Unpaid finals aren’t an office problem — they’re every remaining member’s problem. In a member-owned corporation, every written-off final bill is absorbed by the neighbors who stayed and paid. Recovering them isn’t aggressive — it’s fair.

Southwest Recovery Services is a Texas-based agency working utility receivables on pure contingency since 2004 — and we serve water supply corporations across Texas today. The age curve

Why final bills recover best when placed fresh

Look at the two water supply corporations above: finals placed steadily recovered 55 cents on the dollar; a backlog placed after it had aged recovered 18. The balance didn’t change — the trail did. Every month a final sits, the former member settles somewhere new, the contact data goes stale, and the balance competes with newer bills.

The age curve

Fresh finals (placed within ~90–120 days of move-out) resolve at several times the rate of finals left a year or more.

The standing rule

A simple “place finals at 90 days” policy — run monthly from your billing export — recovers materially more than an annual cleanup, and on contingency there is no cost to placing.
